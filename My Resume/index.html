The Artist

What makes me paint is color. A color can bring about a feeling and emotion like the melody or lyrics of a song. It wasnâ€™t until well into my twenties that I first discovered painting. Growing up writing music, I drew many parallels to the soothing nature of placing a brush stroke on canvas to the creative aspect of writing a note to the melody of a song.

While a song puts music to the words around me, my paintings convey a melody that no words can fully capture. And thus, I discovered the beauty of painting-- as a way to express those emotions and experiences that fall just beyond the tip of my tongue. I tend to paint when I cannot find words to express how I feel; and while I do love expressing myself in words, my paintings often reflect the darker, more intimate shades of my soul that my words cannot.

Growing up in Boston, New York City, and Los Angeles, I draw from many experiences and colorful lifestyles to create what I see and feel around me. People, especially those close around me, inspire me and so I naturally gravitate toward portraiture; but there is a whimsical, dreamlike quality to my work that is often ominous in nature. This quality weaves through many of my paintings like a melody weaves through a song. I draw inspiration from such artists as Van Gogh, Picasso, and my real life friends.. They are perhaps my most influential source of inspiration and raw emotion.

The following pieces represents the last few years of my life, when painting was the only clear way I could express myself. From the tragedy of a fire, to the muse of friends, to the wonderful moments of weekends on Cape Cod, these are the moments that have shaped me and inspired me as an artist.

I hope you enjoy my work.
